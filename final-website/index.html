Introduction

The critically acclaimed five-star restaurant “Starchy” is know far and wide for its creative and mouthwatering potato themed dishes, fondly nicknamed by regulars as being the “Starchest Restaurant” ever. One evening, a suspicious ingredient shipment is delivered to the restaurant from an unknown farm. When the ingredients, freshly harvested potatoes, are used in the next day's meals - the unthinkable happens. The potatoes come to life and start a revolution against the restaurant staff. Take on the role of a young waiter determined to eradicate each and every potato. With all these potatoes roaming the halls, suddenly this lovely restaurant has become more like a dungeon. The “Starchest” Dungeon . . .



Starchest Dungeon is a game inspired by the dungeon crawler genre of gaming; games such as Zelda, Moonlighter, and Enter the Gungeon. We as a team wanted to replicate the feeling of having to explore rooms filled with hostile enemies with an ultimate goal of defeating a main boss. Artistically, we took inspiration from a Metroidvania named Hollow Knight. This art style was translated into a top down format and helped give our game more personality.

We wanted to create an environment where the player had to interact with the potato enemies in various ways. The potatoes were both the player's target and the source of the player's firearm ammunition. This would in turn cause the player to think about their resources, casuing them to think about the strategy of their attacks.
